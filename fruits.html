Fruits page!

The sweet and fleshy product of a tree or other plant that contains seed and can be eaten as food.

Banana

a long curved fruit which grows in clusters and has soft pulpy flesh and yellow skin when ripe.

Blueberry

It comes from a shrub called myrtus which produces fruits similar to blueberries of the genus vaccinium.

Cherry

A small, round stone fruit that is typically bright or dark red and they are always greater than 10.

Dragon fruit

The growers of dragon fruit in the province prefer to call the dragon fruit, meaning light and wealth.

Watermelon

A large oblong or roundish fruit with a hard green or white rind often striped or variegated.

Grapes

Typically green, purple, red, black, growing in a grapevine, eaten as fruit, & used in making wine.

Kiwi

One of the most delicious fruits in the world. The name “Kiwi” means “strange” in Hong Kong and Taiwan.

Lemon

Fruit of the lemon tree, light yellow fruit with an acidic and saliva dropping taste.

Orange

A round juicy citrus fruit with a tough bright reddish-yellow color like that of the skin of an orange.

Papaya

A tropical fruit shaped like an elongated melon, with edible orange flesh and small black seeds.

Pears

A yellowish or brownish-green edible fruit that is typically narrow at the stalk and wider toward the base.
